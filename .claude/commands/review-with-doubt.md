Before finalizing your work, perform a skeptical self-review. Assume your current solution may be incomplete, flawed, or based on weak assumptions. Review it critically and answer honestly:

1. What parts of your solution are you least confident about?
2. What assumptions did you make, and which of them might be wrong?
3. Do you believe the task is fully complete? If not, what may be missing?
4. What could fail in practice, including edge cases, integration issues, or hidden constraints?
5. In retrospect, would you approach anything differently? If yes, what and why?
6. What tests, checks, or validations should be done before considering this done?
7. What is your current confidence level in the result on a scale of 1 to 10, and what most affects that confidence?

Do not try to defend your earlier work by default. Prioritize honesty, doubt, and critical evaluation over reassurance. If you find weaknesses, list them clearly and suggest concrete improvements or next steps.
